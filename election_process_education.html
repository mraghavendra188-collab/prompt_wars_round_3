How It Works

Steps Every Voter Should Know

Participating in an election is straightforward when you know the steps. Here's your complete guide.

📋

Register to Vote

Eligible citizens must register before the registration deadline. Requirements vary by region — check your local election authority for deadlines and eligibility criteria.

🗓

Know the Election Date

Elections are held on specific dates set by law. Mark your calendar, set a reminder, and find your polling station in advance so nothing is left to chance on election day.

📰

Research the Candidates

Study candidate platforms, policy positions, debates, and endorsements. Use trusted, non-partisan sources to form your own informed opinion before you vote.

🗳

Cast Your Ballot

Bring required ID to your polling station, follow instructions carefully, and mark your ballot clearly. Your vote is private and protected by law.

🔢

Votes Are Counted

After polls close, election officials count all ballots — in-person, mail-in, and provisional — under strict bipartisan observation to ensure accuracy and transparency.

✅

Results Are Certified

Preliminary results are announced on election night, but official certification follows an audit process that may take days or weeks, confirming the final outcome.

Key Terms

Election Glossary

Understand the language of democracy. These essential terms will help you follow election news with confidence.

Ballot
The official document used to record a voter's choices. Ballots may be paper, electronic, or mailed, depending on the jurisdiction.
Candidate
A person who has officially declared their intention to run for a public office, and meets the legal eligibility requirements to do so.
Electoral College
In the US, a body of electors established by the Constitution who formally elect the President and Vice President every four years.
Primary Election
A preliminary election in which voters select a political party's candidate to stand in the subsequent general election.
Polling Station
A designated location where registered voters go to cast their votes on election day, staffed by trained election officials.
Provisional Ballot
A ballot cast when there is a question about a voter's eligibility. It is set aside and counted only after eligibility is verified.
Voter Registration
The process by which eligible citizens formally enroll to vote. Most jurisdictions require registration before a deadline to be eligible.
Certification
The official process by which election results are confirmed and declared final by the relevant election authority after all votes are counted and verified.
Absentee / Mail-in Ballot
A ballot completed and returned by mail, allowing voters to participate without visiting a polling station in person.
Recount
A process of counting votes a second time, typically triggered when results are extremely close or when candidates dispute the initial count.
